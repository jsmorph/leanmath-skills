## Why this looks worth deeper analysis

This is not just another math course repo. It looks like a particularly good candidate for `leanmath-skills` because it sits in a useful middle layer between beginner tutorials and frontier formalization work.

What makes it interesting:

- It is explicitly combinatorics-focused, which is one of the target domains.
- It is organized as teaching material with chapter-level Lean files, examples, and exercises, so it may expose reusable proof patterns in a form that is easier to mine than a research repo.
- The README says it is adapted from both *Mathematics in Lean* and the `Lean for the Curious Mathematician 2023` workshop, which suggests it may encode community-tested pedagogy rather than one person's private style.
- It may be a good complement to `YaelDillies/cam-combi`: `cam-combi` is a deeper live research map, while `epfl-comb` may be better for beginner-to-intermediate combinatorics onboarding and exercise-driven skill notes.

## Likely payoff for skill authoring

Potential downstream uses if the internals are good:

- combinatorics onboarding notes
- examples of how graph/combinatorics definitions are introduced to newcomers
- exercise-sized theorem discovery workflows
- comparison of pedagogical definitions with current mathlib structures
- a possible source of small worked examples for future skill docs
