# YaelDillies/cam-combi looks worth deeper analysis

Date: 2026-04-16
Link: https://github.com/YaelDillies/cam-combi

## Why this stands out

This is one of the best combinatorics-specific leads so far.
It is not just a theorem dump. It is a living project with explicit structure for course transcripts, theory development, mathlib-upstreaming, and archived alternative proofs.
That combination makes it unusually likely to yield reusable material for `leanmath-skills`.

## Strong signals

- It is centered on exactly the right domains: graph theory, extremal combinatorics, probabilistic combinatorics, Ramsey theory, additive combinatorics, and growth in groups.
- It documents a deliberate split between lecture-facing files, general theory-development files, and Mathlib-facing prerequisite files meant for upstreaming.
- It names active targets that matter for domain coverage, including Erdős–Rényi random graphs, the Littlewood-Offord problem, the van den Berg–Kesten–Reimer inequality, approximate subgroups, and model-theoretic stability links to additive combinatorics.
- It records what has already been upstreamed, which is useful for separating stable Mathlib knowledge from still-local project material.
- It also preserves archived longer proofs after Mathlib adopts shorter ones, which could be useful when authoring explanatory skill material rather than only minimal final proofs.

## Why this matters for skill authoring

Likely downstream targets:

- combinatorics domain map notes
- upstreaming workflow notes for domain projects that straddle project-local files and Mathlib PRs
- probabilistic-combinatorics API and notation notes
- examples of when a lecture proof gets replaced by a more general Mathlib proof
- guidance on mining project repos for material that has partly upstreamed and partly not
